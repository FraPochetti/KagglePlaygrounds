 
 = Ed Barrow = 
 
 Edward Grant Barrow ( May 10 , 1868 – December 15 , 1953 ) was an American manager and front office executive in Major League Baseball . He served as the field manager of the Detroit Tigers and Boston Red Sox . He served as business manager ( de facto general manager ) of the New York Yankees from 1921 to 1939 and as team president from 1939 to 1945 , and is credited with building the Yankee dynasty . Before his tenure as the Yankees' general manager, Barrow was a minor-league team president (1920-25) and appeared on the executive committee that selected players for the 1920 and 1921 World Series teams. Before serving as the president of the Red Sox, Barrow served as the Detroit Tiger general manager from 1921 to 1935. He was part of a three-year ownership group that also included Dave Roberts, Mel Ott and George C. Batchelder. Barrow was known for his extensive holdings in the Tigers organization and was a member of the management staff of the Yankees and Red Sox. Barrow eventually sold the Tigers to the National League in 1956, but managed the team for several more years. Prior to founding the Chicago White Sox, Barrow also served as the field manager of the Newark Barons of the New Jersey Miners League. Barrow married Ruth Prymer at the age of 34 and they had four sons, Lloyd, H.P. Albert, Joe and Raymond. The popular son, Lloyd Barrow, was an outfielder with the Red Sox and White Sox before his marriage. Barrow died of stomach cancer on December 15, 1953 in New York City.
