 Arthur <unk> " Art " Ross ( January 13 , 1885 â€“ August 5 , 1964 ) was a Canadian professional ice hockey player and executive from 1905 until 1954 . Regarded as one of the best defenders of his era by his peers , he was one of the first to skate with the puck up the ice rather than pass it to a forward . He was on Stanley Cup championship teams twice in a playing career that lasted thirteen seasons ; in January 1907 with the <unk> Thistles and 1908 with the Montreal Wanderers . He was a member of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) from 1913 to 1956 and was promoted to superintendent in February 1937. He won the 1908 Montreal Olympics Hockey Player of the Year Trophy with a team that finished seventh in the preliminary rounds and finished fourth in the Finals. He also won the Conn Smythe Trophy, given to the NHL's best defenseman, in April 1909 and the 1924 Jennings Trophy, given to the best defenseman in the NHL, in August 1924. He was inducted into the Canadian Sports Hall of Fame in October 2013 and of his many awards, the Stanley Cup remains the most recognized sports trophy in the world. His final career (1954-1966) hockey team won the Presidents' Trophy three times in a row. Arthur passed away on August 5, 1964, and is buried at Glenside Cemetery in Brampton, Ontario.
