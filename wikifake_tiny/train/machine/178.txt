 
 = Hurricane Tanya ( 1995 ) = 
 
 Hurricane Tanya was the final storm of the very active 1995 Atlantic hurricane season . The twenty @-@ first tropical cyclone , nineteenth named storm , and eleventh hurricane of the season , Tanya developed from a tropical wave while well north of the Lesser Antilles on October 26 . The system headed northeastward and strengthened into Tropical Storm Tanya on October 27 . It made landfall near Cebu at 8 pm local time, strengthening to a tropical storm and eventual hurricane by the afternoon. It strengthened to a tropical storm again on the night of October 29. 
Tanya set a new surface pressure (18.26 mb or 546.82 kPa) and maximum wind speed (41.2 mph or 73.7 kph) records for the region, following the previous records set in 1990, 1991, 1994, and 1995. The system became the ninth hurricane to be a tropical storm during the 1994-1995 hurricane season. Tanya became the second hurricane to pass through the Lesser Antilles in 1995, the first since Hurricane Patria in 1996. It became the second Category 4 (hurricane strength > 60 mph) hurricane to emerge in the eastern Caribbean region in 1995. Tanya was the fourth hurricane to have a hurricane-force wind gust of at least 60 mph since 1895, following hurricanes Hughie (1948), Ignacio (1959), and Chetanan (1962). Hurricane Patria (1983), the first hurricane to form in the Lesser Antilles, formed near Puerto da Cruz Island, Peru, and achieved a hurricane-force wind gust of at least 70 mph.
