 
 = 1939 Pacific hurricane season = 
 
 The 1939 Pacific hurricane season ran through the summer and fall of 1939 . Before the satellite age started in the 1960s , data on east Pacific hurricanes are extremely unreliable . Most east Pacific storms are of no threat to land . However , 1939 saw a large number of storms threaten California . 
 
 = = Storms = = 
 
 
 = = = Hurricane One = = = 
 
 On June 12 , a hurricane was detected . The storm began a 4-day rain parade that lasted up to two weeks. On June 13, the storm grew to strength of category 4 strength and cyclone-force winds that reached winds up to 155 miles per hour. The storm caused flooding, extreme wind damage, and downed power lines.

 On August 17, this hurricane made landfall at Ventura County, California as a tropical storm. 

 

On August 19, this hurricane spun out of a north to south northwesterly direction over the North Pacific. It began weakening as it left the north pacific and headed westward along the western coast of the United States. 

 

On August 24, this hurricane made landfall near Los Angeles, California. The storm's strongest winds reached 155 miles per hour and the storm moved to a maximum sustained wind speed of about 145 miles per hour. This hurricane produced a maximum sustained wind of 79 miles per hour and a central pressure of 921 millibars. The hurricane made landfall in the city of San Francisco. 

 

On August 25, the storm made landfall in San Jose, California as a tropical storm. This hurricane caused flooding, the largest single storm surge in United States history and power outages for 2 days in San Jose, California. As of August 26, this hurricane had strengthened to a tropical storm and was a remnant category 5 storm.

 

On August 27, this tropical storm dumped a storm surge of 14 feet at San Jose and several other coastal cities.
