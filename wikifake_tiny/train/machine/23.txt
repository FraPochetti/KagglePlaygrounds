 
 = Ward Churchill = 
 
 Ward <unk> Churchill ( born 1947 ) is an American author and political activist . He was a professor of ethnic studies at the University of Colorado Boulder from 1990 until 2007 . The primary focus of his work is on the historical treatment of political dissenters and Native Americans by the United States government . His work features controversial and provocative views , written in a direct , often <unk> style . 
Kenneth M. Kizer Jr. was born in 1988 in Austin, Texas, and was educated at Austin Central Community College and the University of Texas, Austin, USA. He has also held a number of academic positions in both the social sciences and the humanities. He received his BA in Philosophy from the University of Texas, Austin in 1991, with a concentration in American Philosophy, and an MA in Modern Philosophy from the University of Texas, Austin in 1994. Kizer attended the Vanderbilt University, Nashville, as a Fulbright Scholar in 1993. He currently lives in Austin, Texas. He is editor of I Life In America, which is devoted to personal reflections on the life, time, and environment of Americans. The book was published in September 2013 in New York by Princeton University Press. Kizer is a recipient of the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation and the 2017 Dayne Godfrey Memorial Memorial Foundation.
