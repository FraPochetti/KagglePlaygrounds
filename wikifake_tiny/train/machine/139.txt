 
 = Chris Turner ( American football ) = 
 
 Chris Turner ( born September 8 , 1987 ) is an American football quarterback . He played quarterback for the Maryland Terrapins at the University of Maryland from 2007 to 2009 . Turner began his career at Maryland as a redshirt in 2005 and then served as a reserve quarterback before he earned the starting position . 
 After a redshirt season in 2005 , Turner saw no playing time as the third @-@ string quarterback the following year . During the 2006 season, at 5-foot-9, 220 pounds, he posted the best single game statistics in the nation, completing 145 of 201 passes for 1,185 yards with 15 touchdowns and two interceptions. Turner earned M&T's Coach of the Year Award, and was named the 2006 Go Daddy MVP for his stellar play at the position. The previous season, in 2007, at age 24, Turner joined his fellow Terrapin quarterbacks Steve McNair and Terry Housler as the first-team all-SEC selections, and he made one more appearance in the FCS playoffs, as a junior starter in the 2008 Orange Bowl. Turner returned to the team as an assistant coach in 2008 after his senior year of college, coaching the quarterbacks in the 2009 Holiday Bowl against NC State. He did not make an appearance on the offensive or defensive strength of teams in the 2009 season, as he became the fifth NFL quarterback to start for the San Francisco 49ers in the 2011 season. While at Maryland, Turner led the Terrapins to three straight 5-7 records, reaching the College Football Playoff in 2007 and 2009. Turner was named to the John Mackey Award watch list in 2008, which recognized the top returning college football players and motivators.  
Christopher Turner was born in Brooklyn, New York, to Christopher and Marchese Wakefield. Chris is of English and German descent. He graduated from the College of Charleston in 2008 and received a bachelor of science degree in sports management from the University of Maryland in 2009.
