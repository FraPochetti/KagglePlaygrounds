 David Michael Sisler ( October 16 , 1931 â€“ January 9 , 2011 ) was a professional baseball pitcher who played in Major League Baseball ( MLB ) from 1956 through 1962 . Early in his career , Sisler was a starter , then later was used as a middle reliever and occasionally as a closer . He reached the majors in 1956 with the Boston Red Sox after he completed a two @-@ year obligation in the active military . Sisler also played for the San Francisco Giants in 1957, Philadelphia Phillies in 1958, Los Angeles Dodgers in 1959, and the San Diego Padres in 1960.


Sisler pitched at the AAA level for the Rochester Red Wings from 1958 to 1959, but no longer appeared in any regular-season games. He went 1-1 with a 4.16 ERA in eight appearances with the Los Angeles Dodgers in 1961, but made only one start for the Dodgers. During the 1961 offseason, Sisler signed a minor league contract with the San Diego Padres and made the MLB rotation that year.


Sisler started 27 games in the 1961 season, finishing with a 4.18 ERA, 1.06 WHIP, and 26 strikeouts in 20.1 innings pitched. His season was cut short by an elbow injury, and he pitched only once in 1964 before his retirement. Sisler retired as a Major League pitcher in 1968.
