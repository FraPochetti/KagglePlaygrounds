 Daniel Jacob <unk> ( born 23 July 1989 ) is an English actor who rose to prominence as the title character in the Harry Potter film series . He made his acting debut at 10 years of age in BBC One 's 1999 television film David Copperfield , followed by his cinematic debut in 2001 's The <unk> of Panama . At age 11 , he was cast as Harry Potter in the first Harry Potter film , and starred in the series for 10 years until the release of the eighth and final film in 2011 . He had his first recurring role in the BBC's hit British drama series Doctor Who in 2013, playing the character of professor Tom Riddle. In 2014, he was announced as the final "final set piece" for the final Harry Potter film, Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix. The film is one of the most critically acclaimed films of all time, winning numerous awards, including the 89th Academy Awards in 2016. In 2015, Jacob earned his first nomination for Best Supporting Actor in a British film, for his role as the ghost of Harry Potter. He also appeared in the Netflix original series Orange Is the New Black, which chronicles his time on set of the 2016 film, Oblivion.
