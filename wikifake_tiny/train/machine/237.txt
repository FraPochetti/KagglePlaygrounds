 
 = Mole cricket = 
 
 Mole crickets are members of the insect family <unk> , in the order <unk> ( grasshoppers , locusts and crickets ) . Mole crickets are cylindrical @-@ bodied insects about 3 – 5 centimetres ( 1 @.@ 2 – 2 @.@ 0 in ) long , with small eyes and shovel @-@ like forelimbs highly developed for burrowing . They are present in many parts of the world and where they have been introduced into new regions , may become agricultural pests . The larvae of the mole crickets feed on whatever they can find in the soil in the form of fungi, seeds, and scraps of waste or decaying matter. The best time for the mole crickets to be discovered is after the ground has been saturated with rain water, and before the roots of the plants are in contact with the surface, to ensure they feed on all the minerals that they can reach. They hide in rotting vegetation, decaying logs and sand, under rocks and in rotting wood. The soil test that the pest identification services use are for the following key terms : - primary - secondary - tertiary - tertiary - non-trinatal - antimicrobial - natural
A typical mole crickets nest can contain as many as 22-37 larvae. The larvae move in a series of spiracles on to the soil surface and escape from the colony after about 4 days. As many as 1 million mole crickets can be cultivated each year, at their peak population in the summer.
